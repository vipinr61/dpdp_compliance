What is the DPDP Act?

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 is India’s comprehensive law governing the protection and processing of personal digital data. It aims to empower users and regulate how organizations collect, store, and use personal information.

Key Highlights

  • Scope: Applies to digital personal data collected in India, including by foreign entities handling Indian citizens' data.
  • Consent: Must be free, informed, specific, clear, and revocable.
  • Data Principal Rights: Access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, and consent withdrawal.
  • Data Fiduciary Duties: Ensure data accuracy, security, breach notification, and proper governance.
  • Cross-border Data: Allowed unless specifically restricted by the government.
  • Special Obligations for SDFs: Data Protection Officers, DPIAs, and extra accountability.
